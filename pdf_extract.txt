Step Up and Step Down
To Step Up a die, replace it with the next larger size; to Step Down,
replace it with the next smaller. The sizes run d4, d6, d8, d10, d12. A
die never rises above d12 or falls below d4, and a step past either limit
has no effect.

Contested Checks
A Check is contested when two or more characters or parties compete.
Each one makes a Check, adding any modifiers that apply, and the
highest total wins. On a tie, ignore the results and repeat until
someone wins.

Resting
Resting restores Hit Points, Energy, and Luck Dice. It is also when
characters spend Skill Points to advance (see Advancement). There
are two kinds: Camp, a short rest, and Town, a longer rest in a
settlement.
Downtime
Downtime is what a character does during a rest, apart from
recovering. It comes in two tiers. Minor Downtime is short and light:
talking, planning, or light practice, with no mechanical cost. Major
Downtime takes a longer, dedicated stretch and includes Training.
Camp
The Storyteller decides when the party can Camp. A Camp costs
nothing, and every character present gains its benefits.
Each character restores Hit Points equal to half their Max HP and
Energy equal to half their Max Energy, up to their maximum. A
character benefits from a Camp only once in a 24 hour period.
A Camp allows Minor Downtime.
At the end of a Camp, each character may roll their ⟪Charm⟫ die
once to restore one spent Luck Die; the value rolled becomes that
Luck Die.
Town
A Town represents a full day. Each character restores Hit Points and
Energy to full.
A Town allows Major Downtime.
At the end of a Town, each character rolls their ⟪Charm⟫ die three
times, replacing all their Luck Dice.
Training
Training is a Major Downtime activity that earns Skill Points. A
character who trains for a full week, doing nothing else that week,
earns 2 Skill Points. Training requires no roll.
